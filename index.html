About

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Geography at Syracuse University, specializing in urban justice, water security, and climate change. My research employs intersectional, feminist political ecology frameworks to analyze critical issues in South Asian cities, with a particular focus on Dhaka, Bangladesh.

Through my academic work, published opinion pieces, and the Just Urban podcast, I explore the intersections of formal and informal power structures, vulnerability, risk, and environmental justice in urban contexts from the Global North to the Global South.

Research Interests

Urban Justice

Examining how formal and informal power structures shape vulnerability, risk, and access to resources in urban environments.

Water Security

Analyzing water access, governance, and crises in rapidly urbanizing contexts, with focus on peri-urban and informal settlements.

Climate Change

Exploring the intersection of climate change, urban development, and social justice in South Asian cities.

Feminist Political Ecology

Applying intersectional, feminist frameworks to understand environmental and social transformations in urban spaces.
